You can learn a lot about a painting by simply taking photographs with different types of light. Visible light sits within the 'electromagnetic spectrum'. This is a contnuous range of wavelengths that ecmpass different types of radiation, from very high energy, short wavelength gamma radiation down to low energy, long wavelength microwave and radio waves. Figure one shows where visible light sits on the electromagnetic spectrum; different colours of light have different wavelengths, red being the longest and violet the shortest. (This is why when white light is split up into a rainbow through refraction red is always on the top, and blue always on the bottom).
